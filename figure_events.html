The Premier Competitions in the World of Figure Skating

Major Figure Skating Competitions

Figure skating features a diverse calendar of prestigious competitions at both national and international levels. These events showcase the pinnacle of athletic achievement and artistic expression in the sport, from the Olympic stage to annual championship events.

Did you know? The International Skating Union (ISU) governs all international figure skating competitions, establishing rules, judging standards, and competition formats that ensure fairness and consistency across events worldwide.

Winter Olympics

The ultimate honor in figure skating, held every four years as part of the Winter Olympic Games. Skaters compete for their countries in four disciplines: men's singles, women's singles, pairs, and ice dance. The Olympic figure skating competition has produced some of the sport's most memorable moments and legendary athletes.

World Championships

The most prestigious annual competition in figure skating, typically held in March. The World Championships determine the best skaters in each discipline for the season. Qualification is based on national quotas earned through previous year's results, making entry highly competitive.

World Junior Championships

The premier event for skaters aged 13-19 (men) and 13-19 (women), showcasing the next generation of skating stars. Many Olympic champions first gained international recognition at this event. Features the same disciplines as senior competitions with slightly modified technical requirements.

European Championships

The oldest ISU championship (first held in 1891), open to skaters from European member nations. Traditionally dominated by Russian, French, and Italian skaters, this January event serves as an important benchmark before the World Championships.

Four Continents Championships

Established in 1999 as an equivalent to Europeans for non-European countries (North America, Asia, Africa, and Oceania). Provides valuable international experience for skaters from developing skating nations and serves as a key competition in the Olympic qualification process.

Grand Prix Series (Senior and Junior)

A six-event series held each fall featuring the world's top skaters. Senior events include: Skate America, Skate Canada, Cup of China, NHK Trophy (Japan), Internationaux de France, and Rostelecom Cup (Russia). Skaters earn points to qualify for the Grand Prix Final in December. The Junior series follows a similar format for developing skaters.

ISU Challenger Series

Introduced in 2014, this series of international competitions provides additional opportunities for skaters to earn world standing points and prize money. Events include the Nebelhorn Trophy, Finlandia Trophy, and Golden Spin of Zagreb, among others. Often used by skaters to test new programs early in the competitive season.

For the most current information about figure skating competitions, schedules, and results, visit the ISU official website.

Competition Format: Most senior international competitions feature two segments - the Short Program (2 minutes 40 seconds) with required elements, and the Free Skate (4 minutes for women, 4½ minutes for men) that allows more creative freedom. The combined score determines final placements.
