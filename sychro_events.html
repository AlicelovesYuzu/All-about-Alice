Synchronized Skating Events

Synchronized skating competitions showcase the precision, teamwork, and athleticism of teams consisting of 8-20 skaters performing intricate formations and maneuvers in unison. Governed by the International Skating Union (ISU), these events have grown significantly in popularity worldwide.

Major International Events

ISU World Synchronized Skating Championships

  • Established: 2000
  • Frequency: Annual
  • Teams: 24 senior teams from ISU member nations
  • Programs: Short Program (2:50 min) and Free Skate (4:30 min)
  • Recent Champions: Les Suprêmes (CAN), Helsinki Rockettes (FIN), Team Surprise (SWE)

ISU World Junior Synchronized Skating Championships

  • Established: 2013
  • Frequency: Annual
  • Teams: Junior teams (ages 13-19) from ISU member nations
  • Programs: Short Program (2:50 min) and Free Skate (4:00 min)
  • Recent Champions: Team Fintastic (FIN), Les Suprêmes (CAN), Skyliners (USA)

Other Significant Events

ISU Challenger Series in Synchronized Skating

  • Series of international competitions serving as qualifiers for World Championships
  • Includes events like French Cup, Mozart Cup, and Spring Cup
  • Provides competitive experience for developing teams

ISU Synchronized Skating Development Events

  • Designed to promote the sport in developing nations
  • Includes basic novice and intermediate categories
  • Focus on skill development and international exchange

Event Calendar 2024-2025

The ISU synchronized skating season typically runs from November through April, with major events including:

  • November: ISU Challenger Series begins
  • January-February: National Championships worldwide
  • March: World Junior Synchronized Skating Championships
  • April: World Synchronized Skating Championships

New for 2024-2025: The ISU has introduced a new Mixed Age category to encourage participation across generations, and has updated the music requirements for short programs to include more contemporary selections.
