Figure Skating Rules

Figure skating competitions are governed by a comprehensive set of rules established by the International Skating Union (ISU). These rules define the technical requirements, scoring system, and competition formats for all disciplines of figure skating.

Key Rule Changes for 2024-2025 Season: The ISU has implemented adjustments to jump base values, introduced new spin positions, and modified the choreographic sequence requirements in single skating. Pair skating now has stricter lift duration limits, while ice dance has updated pattern dance requirements.

Program Elements by Discipline

Single Skating Elements

  • Jumps: Axel, Lutz, Flip, Loop, Salchow, and Toe Loop - each with different take-off edges and rotations (single, double, triple, or quadruple)
  • Spins: Upright spins, sit spins, camel spins, and combination spins with variations in positions and changes of foot
  • Step Sequence: A series of steps and turns in a pattern across the ice demonstrating technical skill and musical interpretation
  • Choreo Sequence: A sequence of movements and poses expressing the character of the music without specific technical requirements

Pair Skating Elements

  • Group Lift: The man lifts the partner overhead with specific positions and rotations
  • Lift in Lasso: A more difficult lift where the woman is rotated overhead in a lasso-like motion
  • Twist Lift: The man throws the woman into the air where she completes rotations before being caught
  • Throw Jumps: The man assists the woman in a jump where she lands independently
  • Death Spirals: The man pivots while holding the woman's hand as she leans backward in a low position
  • Pair Spin: Both skaters rotate together in various positions while maintaining close proximity
  • Step Sequence: Performed in unison or mirroring each other with intricate footwork

Ice Dance Elements

  • Pattern Dance Elements: Required sequences of steps that follow specific rhythms and patterns
  • Lift: Unlike pairs, ice dance lifts have restrictions on overhead positions and duration
  • Spin: Performed as a team with emphasis on unison and flow rather than difficult positions
  • Steps: Intricate footwork sequences demonstrating technical precision and musicality
  • Twizzle: Traveling turns on one foot with continuous rotation, performed in unison
  • Choreographic Elements: Creative movements highlighting the team's interpretation of the music

ISU Judging System

The International Judging System (IJS) is used to score all ISU competitions. It consists of two main components:

Technical Elements Score (TES)

Each element is assigned a base value determined by its difficulty. Judges then award a Grade of Execution (GOE) ranging from -5 to +5, which is weighted according to the element's base value.

Program Components Score (PCS)

Skaters receive scores in five categories that evaluate the overall quality of the performance:

  • Skating Skills
  • Transitions
  • Performance/Execution
  • Composition
  • Interpretation of the Music
Program Type Minimum Elements Duration Scoring Components
Short Program 7 elements 2:40 (±10 sec) TES + PCS
Free Skate 12 elements 4:00 (women), 4:30 (men) TES + PCS
